RF Information Service programme is linking information / knowledge seekers (farmers, fisher folk, micro-entrepreneur, etc.) and knowledge-holders (universities, research institutions, extension department, individual experts – grassroots resource persons, policy makers, innovative practitioners, journalists, traders, financial institutions, etc.) through multiple interactive platforms for knowledge sharing / broadcasting value addition advisories / getting feedback about the advisories and various services to address the rural development issues / livelihood security issues.

At present, the primary focus is to cover the maximum possible audience in each set of livelihood - major crop cultivators, livestock rearers, marine fisher folk, aquaculture, micro-enterprises, etc., to provide demand driven information such as pest and disease management, vaccination details for livestock, seed treatment, new cultivation methods, government schemes / entitlements, ocean state forecast, availability of fish shoal, financial institutions support schemes, civic services, education, health, employment news, etc. For executing this the methodology is to focus on identifying a small group of individuals of different livelihood with similar information needs for periodical need assessment, collecting feedback and content verification. This process leads to refine and package content. This packaged content will be broadcast through different communication media to communities with similar information needs located in different parts of same districts, the neighbouring districts and across the entire State.
